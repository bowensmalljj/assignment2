Pandemic Unmasked: Navigating COVID-19


COVID-19 around the world





Based on the chart above, you'll find a world map displaying COVID-19 statistics. It shows the number of COVID-19 Deaths and Confirmed Cases per 10,000 people for each country. Some countries are highlighted in dark red to signify high confirmed cases while light orange is represent low confirmed cases. Notably, countries like Chile, Argentina, Peru, Colombia, Spain, and Oman have a high number of confirmed cases per 10,000 people. However, remember that these numbers can be misleading without considering the population size of each country.






Relationship between COVID-19 Confirmed and Deaths Cases






The scatterplot indicates a clear connection between the number of confirmed COVID-19 cases and the number of COVID-19 related deaths in different countries. Specifically, it shows that countries with a higher number of confirmed cases tend to have a higher number of deaths, suggesting a positive relationship between confirmed cases and deaths. Notably, the size of a country's population, such as in the cases of China and India, doesn't necessarily correlate with having the highest number of cases or deaths. To mitigate any potential misinterpretation, it's essential to normalize the data, adjusting for population size. The United States has the highest number of both confirmed cases and deaths, while Western Sahara has the lowest figures for both metrics.
